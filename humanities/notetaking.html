How to Read and Analyze Articles for Remembering Them

7/27/10: I read a PDF called “How To Read” by Paul Edwards.

  • Reading (or doing anything mentally taxing) for more than an hour is impossible! Read a book in several short sessions, rather than one long marathon. When coming back, try to recall what you learned before and what you’re hopefully going to learn next.
    • Read a book/article 3 times (pg 4)
    • First for overview and knowledge of what it will be about and what sub-topics you’re most interested in.
    • Secondly for detail and understanding.
    • Thirdly, for marking up text and making mental notes on how to do things. If this is digital, you’ll be able to instantly search your computer and look at it!
  • Try to take notes on the article itself in PDF or other digital form for better searching later. Not sure if Preview is good enough for this yet, but splitting the screen seems to work pretty well…
  • Use multiple modes of memory
  • I think I learn visually best, so try and remember by drawing pictures.
