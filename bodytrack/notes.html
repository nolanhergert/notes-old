Accelerometer

  • Validated Caloric Expenditure Estimation using a Single Body-Worn Sensor
    • Much better caloric intake estimation than FitBit because they compute activity based on footsteps from the accelerometer data. Intensity is based on speed change derived from footsteps and elevation change, which they compare a pressure sensor and GPS.
    • A jog only burns <500 calories…whereas just sitting or sleeping all day/night adds up quickly(1500-2000 calories)!
  • A simple method for reliable footstep detection (Ryan Libby from UbiComp group at UW)
    • Uses frequency domain to adaptively find the right low-pass filter cutoff. Seems to work okay…

  • Parasympathetic Nervous System: High Freq HRV, “rest and digest” response.
  • Common Measurements are
  • Normalized High Frequency Variability (.05 Hz to 1 Hz???): Correlated to parasympathetic nervous system
  • Normalized Low Freq Variability ( to ????): Correlated to sympathetic nervous system
  • LF/HF Ratio: You want parasympathetic nervous system to dominate over sympathetic, and this ratio basically tells you the balance between them. A high ratio is worse, means you're “dead-er”.

IntelleWave (Nerve-Express)

  • Intellewave (Nerve-Express): His theory review paper is 50 pages long?! Cites Marvin Minsky's Frame Theory as inspiration.
    • His patent, Method for Quantitative Assessment of Autonomic Nervous System based on HRV Analysis, goes a little more in-depth
    • $4000 for Zephyr HxM Bluetooth ($100) and his proprietary software
    • Just got a patent too in case the paper doesn't spill the beans.
    • Nerve-Express solved a challenging problem of subdividing the Spectral Function of R-R intervals variability into two parts:
      - High Frequency (which correlates with Parasympathetic activity 95% of the time) and
      - Low Frequency (correlates with Sympathetic activity up to 70% of time).
      In the past, the division line (between PNS and SNS) was found at a mean Frequency of 0.15 Hz (for an average healthy person). However, for each individual, this transition point varies.
      Nerve-Express was able to come up with a calculation which is unique to any given person.
      In addition, Nerve-Express also dealt with the problem of reducing all possible variations of multiple parameters (20-30) into a quantitative relationship between only two parameters: SNS and PSNS. Nerve-Express is the first and only system to solve this problem of SNS-PSNS quantification. This technological breakthrough is achieved by the application of Dr. Riftine's proprietary algorithms.

Working Out

  • HRV Tips for Not Overtraining
  • This article talks about FirstBeat's research that shows that training programs using HRV data to not push the body too hard and rest accordingly will help you perform better than the normal exercise routine…
  • You can get a very good approximation of your anaerobic threshold when you start breathing hard. It's not as sensitive as actually measuring lactic acid building and VO2 output, but close enough for 99.9% of the population. Academic PaperZephyr Technology White Paper
  • Seems like aerobic is good for short activities and anaerobic is better for quick bursts. Not sure though why/how you would train knowing that!
