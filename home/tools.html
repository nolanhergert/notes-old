Silly Scopes

Maybe I want to use the Saleae? Or a competitor, the DSLogic. good comparison. Definitely want to sell the Rigol as I don't need the bandwidth (right now) and it doesn't have logic analysis built in.

  • Saleae doesn't have external clock, continuous triggering,
  • Another competitor is SmartScope by LabNation
  • Saleae only goes up to 10V on the pro models, 5V on the lesser models.

Might go with the Digilent Analog Discovery 2 instead (up to 25V)?? https://www.sparkfun.com/products/13929

Probably don't need full scope probes for lower frequency signals (cheap Saleae logic analyzer probes are ok). http://electronics.stackexchange.com/a/56145/71181

  • One downside of analog recording functionality of Saleae Logic is that you can't increase the voltage gain, so not great for small signals, <100mv
  • So, getting a full arbitrary function generator is probably a little overkill unless you're really into analog electronics testing. For now, just get a good scope and then look for a signal generator that does what you'd like. Thread on Electronics StackExchange

CircuitGear CGR-101

  • My YouTube Review:YouTube Link! https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=smLCq1QOJjw
  • Doesn't have easy one-shot zooming, moving around and stuff! Which is really lame! Returning…
  • Want to get an oscilloscope/waveform generator/logic analyzer. The CircuitGear CGR-101 seems to be a good option, except it doesn't seem to have a logic analyzer. Waiting to hear back if logic analysis is possible. Software is OPEN SOURCE & WIN/MAC/UBUNTU and quite intuitive for what it does.   • Also their Analog Circuit Design PDF looks really solid w/ good practical examples. Buy when you get their scope?
