Intervalometer

  • Basically a device for manually taking X-second pictures consistently on a camera.
  • Randy would like a way to connect one base unit to multiple cameras to take Gigapan resolution shots at the same exact time.
  • Josh suggested using an optoisolator, which is a very good idea
  • Based on 2 tutorials

TINY German Guy

here

  • Uses tiny PIC and doesn't need external power. Uses a watchdog timer.
  • However, doesn't have connections for additional cameras.
  • “Listens” for button press on camera, WHICH IS SO SLICK, and has 2 diodes to allow voltage from either focus or shutter to power VDD, but not allow them to cross-talk.
  • Adds 22uF capacitor for temporary power, but sometimes causes shutter to depress when pressing in initially

HDR Jack

HDR Jack

  • Another take, uses a button (doesn't listen), and doesn't really have a big capacitor?
  • Input voltage goes to a pin (not VDD), which he says saves power
  • Has HDR mode for taking different shutter exposure timings automagically.

Both use watchdog timers, and I'm thinking that's what I want to do too.
