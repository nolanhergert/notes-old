Analysis

  • What do these masks do to the input image?
    Each mask performs a differentiation on the pixels surrounding it and generally highlights the “edges” of the original image.
  • What are the advantages of using a small mask? Why would you want to use a large mask?
    Using a small mask allows for very high detail, whereas a larger mask blurs over some of the noise in the image because it samples from a larger area but also smooths over sharp edges.
  • Suppose you wanted to identify the horizon in images at sea. What mask might you use to do this?
    The masks used in this experiment are good only for vertically-oriented edges, whereas these masks flipped 90° would perform much better for a horizon at sea and the horizontal edges in these images.
